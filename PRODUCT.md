# Prospect

register: product

## Product purpose

A verified regulatory frontier of human CD4+ T-cell biology. Prospect is a trust layer for
AI-generated science: it holds a linked, signed graph of gene regulation re-derived from released
CRISPRi Perturb-seq data, and it refuses to let a model's claim enter that graph on the model's
word. The interface exists to let a skeptical scientist see what the data actually holds, and to
show the boundary where AI activity becomes verified, replayable state.

## Users

- **Domain experts, skeptical by default.** Immunologists and computational biologists, including
  the lab that produced the data. They can smell a fake finding instantly. They arrive expecting AI
  hand-waving and need to be shown re-derivable evidence, not a confident paragraph.
- **Evaluators.** People judging whether this is real work: they scan fast, want the number and the
  proof, and distrust polish that outruns substance.
- The reader is always in a task: reading a finding, checking a gene, following the trust chain.
  Density is welcome where they need it; decoration is not.

## Tone and brand

Restraint. Concrete before abstract. Trust the reader. The visual language is Japanese aesthetics
(wabi-sabi, kintsugi, ma) meeting scientific cartography: Cajal's line, Hasui's blue hour. A cool
paper canvas, indigo ink, one borrowed-gold accent kept as the soul (the kintsugi seam at
thresholds where provenance changes). The instrument should disappear into the science. Numbers
carry the weight; chrome does not.

## Strategic principles

- The data speaks. Every claim on screen is backed by a re-derivable number or a typed status.
- Status is typed and honest. The interface never says "verified" or "true"; it says reproduced,
  contradicted, evidence-attached, open.
- No model in the trust path. The thing that decides is frozen code and a human key, and the UI
  makes that legible.
- Density where experts need it (tables, gene profiles, adjacency), quiet everywhere else.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS analytics dashboards. Card-grid monotony, everything in a box.
- The AI-tool aesthetic: purple/blue gradients, dark neon glows, glassmorphism, gradient text.
- The hero-metric template (one giant number over supporting stats with a gradient accent).
- ML-leaderboard density-for-its-own-sake. We are not a benchmark site; we are a scientific record.
- Any warmth-by-default cream/beige "premium" palette. The canvas is cool first-light, not cream.
